# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

## Overview

OctoAcme uses a structured, collaborative project management approach grounded in five core principles: **customer-first prioritization**, **iterative delivery**, **clear ownership**, **data-informed decisions**, and **psychological safety**. These principles guide how we initiate, plan, execute, release, and continuously improve our projects.

Our methodology follows a complete project lifecycle divided into five phases:

1. **Initiation** — Validate business need, align stakeholders, and define success metrics through a lightweight Project One-pager.
2. **Planning** — Break work into shippable increments, estimate scope, identify dependencies, and create a prioritized backlog with clear acceptance criteria.
3. **Execution & Tracking** — Build and test iteratively using a structured workflow with daily standups, weekly syncs, and a project board (Backlog, Ready, In Progress, In Review, QA, Done).
4. **Release & Deployment** — Deploy to production following pre-release checklists, security scanning, smoke tests, and rollback planning to minimize risk.
5. **Retrospective & Continuous Improvement** — Capture learnings after each sprint or milestone and convert them into prioritized action items for ongoing refinement.

### Key Practices

**Roles & Responsibilities**: OctoAcme operates with clearly defined roles—Project Managers coordinate delivery and timelines, Product Managers define outcomes and prioritize the backlog, Developers implement features and collaborate on design, and QA validates quality. This clarity prevents ambiguity and empowers ownership.

**Communication & Cadence**: We maintain regular touchpoints including daily standups (15 min), weekly delivery syncs, and monthly stakeholder updates. Status updates, risk registers, and decision logs serve as single sources of truth.

**Quality & Risk Management**: Small pull requests (≤400 lines), automated CI/CD with tests and security scanning, structured risk registers, and escalation paths (Team → PM → Product Lead → Sponsor) ensure consistent quality and transparency.

## Getting Started

1. Review the **Project Management Overview** for context on OctoAcme's approach.
2. When starting a new project, follow the **Project Initiation Guide** to validate and scope the work.
3. Use the **Project Planning** doc to create your backlog and timeline.
4. Reference **Execution & Tracking** during daily work and sprints.
5. Use **Risk Management & Communication** to maintain transparency and escalate blockers.
6. Follow the **Release & Deployment Guide** before shipping to production.
7. After each milestone or release, run a retrospective using **Retrospective & Continuous Improvement**.

For role-specific guidance, consult **Roles and Personas** to understand your responsibilities and how your function fits into the broader project.
